# Vision

## Why

AI proficiency is becoming the dividing line for knowledge workers. Section's
[AI Proficiency Report](https://www.sectionai.com/ai/the-ai-proficiency-report) puts
roughly **95% of the workforce below the proficiency bar**: ~21% Novices, ~73%
Experimenters stuck in one-off usage, and only ~5% Practitioners and Experts who have
embedded AI into repeatable workflows or build them for others.

Numbers don't move people. **Stories do, and images do.** A learning journey rendered
as a living tree — visibly rooted, visibly growing, visibly reaching upward — is a
story you can look at. The goal of this project is to make one person's AI learning
journey (Walter's, first) so tangible and beautiful that other knowledge workers feel
the itch to grow their own.

## For whom

1. **Walter** — dogfooding instance #1. The tree grows from his real GitHub commits
   and Obsidian notes across his real fields of practice (SEO/GEO, content, vibe
   coding, workflow automation …).
2. **Any knowledge worker with a GitHub account** — the end state is a public repo
   anyone can hook up to their own GitHub profile (and optionally an Obsidian vault)
   to grow their own tree.
3. **The audience of each tree** — colleagues, followers, employers. A tree is meant
   to be *shown*: embedded in a blog, shared as a time-lapse clip, walked through
   interactively.

## What "done" feels like (end-state scenes)

- **The reveal.** You open your tree. Camera drifts down through the emergent layer,
  through your canopy, and settles at a gentle orbit. Leaves shimmer where you worked
  this week. It is unmistakably *yours* — nobody else's history would grow this shape.
- **The time-lapse.** One button: *Replay my journey.* The tree grows from seed in 30
  seconds — first a shy stem of early experiments, then the SEO bough thickening,
  then the pro-code limb shooting through the canopy — while the camera orbits and
  the soundtrack swells. This is the clip people share.
- **The level-up.** A branch tip crosses from understory into canopy. It blossoms.
  Chime. The camera swings over to witness it. Proficiency milestones are *events*,
  not spreadsheet cells.
- **The forest (later).** A shared grove where each member of a team or community is
  a tree. You walk between them. You see whose video bough is tall and go ask them
  how they did it. Inspiration becomes spatial.

## Principles

1. **Evidence, not vibes.** The tree grows from verifiable artifacts (commits, notes,
   declared milestones) — it can't be inflated by hand-waving.
2. **Honest but kind.** Dormant areas turn autumnal; they never look dead or punished.
   The tree celebrates growth, it doesn't shame gaps.
3. **Deterministic beauty.** The same history always grows the same tree. Randomness
   is seeded. That makes the journey replayable, comparable over time, and trustworthy.
4. **Private roots, public canopy.** What's public (GitHub) grows above ground. What's
   private (Obsidian) feeds the tree as roots — visible only to the owner, or as an
   anonymous silhouette. Privacy is built into the metaphor itself.
5. **Config, not constants.** Walter's sectors are a default taxonomy, not a law.
   Someone else's tree may need different fields. The taxonomy is data.
6. **The repo is a leaf on its own tree.** This project is itself AI-assisted pro-code
   work — the tree visualizes the making of the tree. Dogfooding from day one.
